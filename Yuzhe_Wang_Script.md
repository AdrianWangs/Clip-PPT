## 2.1 Findings of the Project

"Our research found four main pillars that drive China's AI success. Think of them as four strong legs of a table.

**First pillar is Policy.** The government made a clear plan in 2017. They want China to be the world's AI leader by 2030. They also made programs to train people and manage data.

**Second pillar is Talent.** This is about people who work in AI. China has 47% of the world's top AI researchers. That's almost half! They have 535 universities teaching AI. Companies also train their workers.

**Third pillar is Public Participation.** This means normal people help too. China has over 1 billion internet users. These people create lots of data when they use apps and websites. This data helps train AI systems.

**Fourth pillar is Industry.** These are the companies that make AI products. The AI industry in China is worth 600 billion RMB. Big companies like Baidu, Alibaba, Tencent, and Huawei lead this work.

All four pillars work together. They support each other to make China's AI strong."

---

## 2.2 Preliminary Successes

"Now let me show you the real results of this teamwork.

**First, we see great collaboration.** Shanghai AI Laboratory works with 13 universities. Beijing Academy of AI made a huge model called WuDao with 1.75 trillion parameters. Huawei works with universities all over China.

**Second, we have clear numbers that show success:**
- China produces 47% of the world's top AI researchers
- The AI industry reached 600 billion RMB in 2024
- PaddlePaddle has 18.1 million developers using it
- Over 1 billion users provide data for AI training

**The key point is this:** China uses both smart people and data from regular people. The government and companies work together to make this happen. This creates a powerful engine for AI innovation."

## 3.1 Summary

"Let me summarize our core findings.

China's AI success comes from one main strategy: **focusing on people**. This happens in five ways:

**First:** Policy that puts people first. The government makes plans that help develop human talent.

**Second:** A talent engine with many channels. Universities train students, companies train workers, and China attracts smart people from other countries.

**Third:** Public participation at a massive scale. Regular people help by using apps and creating data that trains AI systems.

**Fourth:** Industry innovation with government support. Private companies lead innovation while the government helps them succeed.

**Fifth:** All parts work together. Government, universities, companies, and people all support each other.

This is not just about technology or money. It's about organizing people in a smart way to build AI together."
